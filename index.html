About

Research Interests & Background

I am a graduate researcher at the University of Pennsylvania, pursuing an MSE in Electrical Engineering with a concentration in Nanotechnology & Semiconductors. My research spans ferroelectric memory devices, MEMS/NEMS fabrication, and analog/mixed-signal IC design. I work at the intersection of device physics, TCAD modelling, and cleanroom nanofabrication — characterizing novel materials like AlScN for next-generation non-volatile memory and fabricating quantum devices at the Singh Center for Nanotechnology.


Prior to Penn, I spent two years as a hardware engineer at Silicon Laboratories designing mixed-signal evaluation boards, and interned at Cadence Design Systems working on 3nm device modelling. I am actively preparing for PhD applications in electrical engineering, with a focus on semiconductor device research.

Synopsys Sentaurus TCAD · Keithley SCS-4200

Wideband Transimpedance Amplifier for Optical Receivers

Cadence Virtuoso · 45nm · Fall 2025

  • 8-stage cascaded TIA achieving 519 kΩ (114 dBΩ) gain, exceeding spec by 3.5×.
  • Maintained 311 MHz bandwidth with a 600Ω differential load.
  • "Direct Coupling" strategy with tapered loading to eliminate AC coupling.
  • Power consumption: 3.5 mW (well below 10 mW budget).

Low-Power 16×4 SRAM Array Design

Cadence Virtuoso · 45nm CMOS · Fall 2025

  • Synchronous SRAM macro: 249 ps (~4 GHz) worst-case write access time.

  • Optimized Area-Power-Delay FOM improvement by 46%.
  • Compact layout: 2.42 µm², 356 µW active power at 1.2V.
