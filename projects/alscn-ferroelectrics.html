Modelling & Characterization of AlScN Ferroelectric Devices

Synopsys Sentaurus TCAD · Keithley SCS-4200 · Aug 2025 – Present

This ongoing research focuses on aluminium scandium nitride (AlScN)-based ferroelectric field-effect transistors (FE-FETs) as candidates for next-generation non-volatile memory. The work spans both TCAD simulation and hands-on electrical characterization.

TCAD Modelling

Modelling AlScN-based FE-FETs in Synopsys Sentaurus TCAD, replacing gate oxides with ferroelectric stacks and matching it with real-life wafer configurations. The simulation framework captures polarization switching, remnant polarization, and coercive field dynamics.

Electrical Characterization

Characterizing ferroelectric memory arrays using the Keithley SCS-4200 semiconductor characterization system. Measurements include pulsed I-V, PUND (Positive-Up Negative-Down) sequences, DC-IV sweeps, and reliability/endurance cycling.

Device Integration

Working toward benchmarking ferroelectric memory performance against conventional charge-trap and floating-gate technologies, with an eye on scaling for cleanroom fabrication.
