Tuning-Fork Vibratory MEMS Gyroscope

ESE 5290 · MEMS Design · 5 μm SOI DRIE · Spring 2026

Architected and numerically synthesized a fully decoupled, mode-split tuning-fork vibratory MEMS gyroscope. Designed for a 5 μm Silicon-on-Insulator (SOI) DRIE process, this project heavily focuses on mitigating real-world fabrication variances—such as critical dimension (CD) over-etch and out-of-plane stress curling—using active electrostatic spring softening and symmetric suspension topologies to guarantee a phase-stable, wideband response.

Key Results

  • Kinematic Optimization — Architected a precise 100 Hz frequency split (fdrive = 15.0 kHz, fsense = 15.1 kHz) using a 200-finger differential comb array to extract a robust 123 pA motional current at 1°/sec rotation, exceeding target resolution.
  • Process Integration — Engineered a custom Poly-Si anchor plug module within the fabrication flow to completely seal the underlying BOX layer, preventing catastrophic structural undercut during the isotropic vapor HF release phase.
  • Yield & Error Mitigation — Modeled active post-fabrication electrostatic spring tuning (via a 15V DC bias) to perfectly counteract up to an 18% mechanical stiffness reduction caused by inherent DRIE Bosch process sidewall over-etching.
